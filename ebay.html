Overview

eBay.com

The goal was to enhance the selling experience for casual sellers on eBay by simplifying the listing process, which was long and tedious, causing many users to abandon it before completing their listings.

Challenges

The existing selling form on eBay was long and tedious, particularly for casual sellers. As a result, many casual sellers were abandoning the process before completing their listings. While the form worked reasonably well for other user groups, it proved to be error-prone and discouraging for casual sellers.

Role

I led the user research, interaction design, testing, and workshops with product managers and developers. My role was to identify the pain points and design a solution that would improve the experience for casual sellers.

Understanding the Problem

To understand why the selling process was so challenging for casual sellers, I collaborated with the UX Research Team to conduct usability tests on the selling form. Despite its effectiveness for other user groups, the form was causing significant drop-offs among casual sellers.

User Research

Together with the UX Research Team, we devised five different test scenarios of varying complexity to identify user pain points. We conducted these sessions to uncover the specific challenges casual sellers were facing.

Findings

The research revealed several key pain points:

  • Length of the Listing Process: Sellers found the listing process too long and cumbersome.
  • Confusion Over Selling Decisions: Users struggled with decisions regarding categories, shipping information, and pricing.
  • Impatience of Casual Sellers: This user group typically has very little patience and prefers a quick and straightforward selling process.
  • Mandatory Fields: The form required many mandatory fields, which further slowed down the process.

Reframing the Challenge

To make the selling process less confusing and more efficient, we needed to cater specifically to casual sellers' needs. We analyzed that casual sellers wanted a quick and easy way to list their items, similar to platforms like Craigslist, where a simple post with a few details is enough to start selling.

User Testing

Given the novelty of the idea, I worked with the research team to test the prototype before presenting it to the broader team. The results were promising: 5 out of 6 participants preferred the new user flow, describing it as refreshing and easy to use.

Co-Design and Refinement

Armed with these positive test results, I led co-design sessions with product managers and developers to refine the design, making it more viable and feasible. We also collaborated with the visual design and development teams to create higher-fidelity wireframes.

Design System Development

The new design introduced components not present in eBay's existing design system, such as the green connection cards. These components were refined and considered for inclusion in other tools.

Outcomes

The new design was launched as a beta version to 30% of eBay users. Within three months, the results were clear:

  • Reduced Abandonment Rates: The number of casual sellers abandoning the process decreased significantly.
  • Increased Listings: There was a noticeable increase in the number of listings from casual sellers.
  • Positive Feedback: Other user groups also responded positively, and there was interest in extending this tool to other product categories.
