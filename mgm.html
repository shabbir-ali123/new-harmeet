Overview

mgm

Business Connect is an on-premise enterprise B2B platform that facilitates secure communication and transfer of messages and documents between different business partners and a central host. The application, initially built over 20 years ago, was in dire need of modernization to keep up with current technological standards and user expectations. I initiated this project alongside a team member and, after establishing a solid foundation, transitioned the project to my team in India for further development. My involvement spanned end-to-end across various phases, working closely with stakeholders, UX researchers, developers, and QA teams.

User Research

Research and Analysis

Our approach began with a thorough analysis of the existing booking flow, focusing on user pain points and drop-off stages. We conducted:

  • User Interviews: To gather insights into user frustrations and expectations.
  • Heuristic Evaluation: To identify usability issues in the current design.
  • Analytics Review: To understand where users were dropping off and why.
  • Competitor Analysis: To benchmark against industry standards and identify potential improvements.

Design Strategy

Based on our research, we developed a design strategy focused on simplifying the user journey and making the booking process more intuitive and mobile-friendly.

Key Design Enhancements

Condensed Calendar View

  • Problem: Users were overwhelmed by too many options in the calendar view.
  • Solution: We redesigned the calendar to present a simplified view with clear, easy-to-select dates. This reduced cognitive load and made the process faster and more intuitive.

Streamlined Seat Selection

  • Problem: The seat selection process was cumbersome and confusing.
  • We introduced a visual layout that allowed users to easily identify and select available seats. By reducing the number of steps and providing clear visual cues, we made the process more user-friendly.

Optimized Seat Reservation

  • Problem: The reservation process required too many clicks, leading to drop-offs.
  • Solution: We simplified the reservation flow, reducing the number of clicks needed to reserve seats. This was achieved by grouping related actions and providing clear instructions at each step.

Simplified Checkout Flow

  • Problem: The checkout process was cluttered and confusing.
  • Solution: We streamlined the checkout process by removing unnecessary steps and distractions. The new design focused on guiding users through payment with minimal interruptions, ensuring a smoother experience.
